大会简介About CMAS

CMAS 2026 将于 2026 年 6 月 27-28 日在南京大学苏州校区举办，由 CCF 主办，CCFAI 和南京大学协办，多智能体学组执行。CMAS 2026 will be held on June 27-28, 2026 at the Suzhou Campus of Nanjing University, hosted by CCF, co-organized by CCFAI and Nanjing University, and executed by the Multi-Agent Systems Group.

About CMASAbout CMAS

大会定位Conference Positioning

CMAS 由国内多智能体系统学界共同建设，持续服务学术交流、人才连接与产学研合作。CMAS is jointly built by the Chinese multi-agent systems community and continues to serve academic exchange, talent connection, and industry-academia collaboration.

大会定位Conference Standing

作为中国多智能体系统领域规模最大、影响力最强的顶级学术盛会，CMAS 已成为全国最具代表性的多智能体学术平台。As the largest and most influential top academic event in China for multi-agent systems, CMAS has become the country's flagship conference in the field.

学术基础Academic Base

多智能体系统学组自 2014 年成立以来，已发展为 CCFAI 专委下规模最大、最活跃的学术组织。Since its founding in 2014, the Multi-Agent Systems Group has grown into the largest and most active academic organization under CCFAI.

双轨生态Dual-Track Ecosystem

CMAS 聚焦国内学术共同体建设，DAI 国际会议则持续拓展国际交流，两者共同构成国内与国际双轨并行的学术高地。CMAS focuses on the domestic academic community, while the DAI conference extends international exchange, together forming a dual-track academic ecosystem.

本届亮点Highlights

本届大会将通过主旨报告、六个分论坛与产学研合作对话，集中展示多智能体系统的最新进展。This edition will showcase the latest progress in multi-agent systems through keynote talks, six forums, and industry-academia dialogue.

主旨报告Keynotes

大会设置开幕式、大会特邀报告与主旨报告，并安排赞助商讲话。The conference features an opening ceremony, invited reports, keynote talks, and sponsor remarks.

六个分论坛Six Forums

六个分论坛覆盖大模型多智能体系统、多智能体强化学习、具身智能、博弈论与机制设计、AI4S 与 AI4Social Science。Six forums cover LLM-based multi-agent systems, multi-agent reinforcement learning, embodied intelligence, game theory and mechanism design, AI4S, and AI4Social Science.

合作邀请Partnership Invitation

大会诚邀合作伙伴共同定义智能未来，展示技术实力、连接顶尖高校人才并推动产学研合作。Partners are warmly invited to define the intelligent future together, showcase technical strength, connect with top talent, and build research-industry collaboration.
